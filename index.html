✓ Recommendation: 3-Vendor Optimal Split — Estimated ~$595,000

Urban for granulation (non-negotiable). The GHL-250 at 80 kg/batch is the only production-scale granulator in this vendor set. Canaan's LHS75 (15–30 kg) and Rich Packing's HLSG-20 (3–8 kg) are both structurally incompatible with downstream production-scale equipment. Urban's granulation line must anchor the order regardless of Part 11 outcome — if Urban fails Part 11, granulation equipment is bought without the computerized system dependency; validated manually with a separate MES layer.

Rich Packing for tablet press and film coater. The HGZ-26D tablet press (26 stations, 165,000/hr, $65,800) offers competitive throughput at reasonable cost. More critically, the BGB-80 coater ($39,500) includes a full WIP system — a GMP-mandatory requirement that Hualian's BG-80 lacks. The WIP gap cannot be negotiated around: it is a cleaning validation architecture issue, not a paperwork issue.

Hualian for capsule filling and blister/cartoning. The NJP-2500 at 150,000 capsules/hr is 2× Rich Packing's NJP-1500D for $5,850 more — justified for scale-up headroom. The DPB-270J blister line ($121,600 complete) is the only cold-form alu-alu option in this vendor set, which is required for moisture-sensitive drugs in Zone IVb tropical markets (Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand). Rich Packing's DPP-270Max is ALU-PVC thermoform only and cannot be converted.

Canaan: Not recommended unless Urban fails Part 11 comprehensively. Canaan's IQ/OQ/DQ documentation and Part 11 compliance are best in class, but the $761,720 price tag with a 6-month lead time and HSM bottleneck is difficult to justify when the 3-vendor split delivers comparable or superior individual equipment at ~$595K.
